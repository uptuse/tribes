2026-04-29 19:25 | 2f4e3cf | fix(R32.37.3-manus): HOTFIX white-fog terrain regression. User R32.37.2 screenshot showed the entire terrain rendered as a uniform grey/white haze — buildings still visible floating in the air, but the painterly ground was completely gone. ROOT CAUSE: my R32.37.1 POM implementation built the parallax view direction from `-vec2(dFdx(vWorldY), dFdy(vWorldY))`, normalized. At distance, the terrain compresses to a thin band of pixels at the horizon, and dFdx/dFdy of vWorldY become huge unstable values; normalizing produces a unit vector pointing in essentially a random direction per fragment. Combined with uPOMScale=0.06 × 10 steps = 0.6 tile-uv units of UV walk per pixel, every distant fragment sampled chaotic off-tile coords, averaging to flat grey across the whole far field. AO + roughness amplified the wash because they ALSO sampled at the corrupt POM-shifted UV (since R32.37.1 reused tUv after POM). REWRITE: (1) POM now uses canonical tangent-space view direction — V_view = normalize(vViewPosition); V_ts = normalize(transpose(tbn) * V_view); maxOffset = (V_ts.xy / max(0.20, abs(V_ts.z))) * uPOMScale * pomFade. tbn and vViewPosition are both auto-injected by Three because the terrain MeshStandardMaterial has normalMap set. (2) Distance fade pomFade = 1 - smoothstep(25, 60, length(vViewPosition)) so POM is full strength under 25m and zero past 60m — visual cost zero where you can't see it anyway, and immune to horizon-derivative blow-up. (3) Hard cap on offset magnitude (`if (mlen > uPOMScale) maxOffset *= uPOMScale/mlen`) as a belt-and-braces guarantee. (4) Grazing-angle clamp (zCos floor 0.20) prevents the V_ts.xy/V_ts.z division from exploding. (5) uPOMScale 0.06 -> 0.025 (≈2.3cm at 9m tiles) — smaller, safer, still visible at close range. (6) uPOMSteps 10 -> 8 (small win on shader cost, raymarch is now bounded so fewer steps suffice). DEFAULTS: ST.pbrPOM and the shader uniform both default to FALSE now, so first load is safe even on stale cached HTML; user can flip the POM chip ON to opt in. Roughness + AO still default ON (their isolated impact is small and well-bounded).
